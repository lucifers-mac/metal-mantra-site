The week refuses to let up. From Sphere expansions to farewell tours that keep getting bigger, here's what's moving in metal right now.

## Metallica Keep Stacking the Las Vegas Sphere Calendar

Six more dates have been added to Metallica's ongoing Las Vegas Sphere residency, and general on-sale kicks off today at 10 AM PST via Ticketmaster. The band continues their no-repeat-setlists approach, which means every night out is a completely different show. If you've been waiting for a reason to finally make the trip to Vegas, the calendar just got longer.

## Venom Announce New Album *Into Oblivion*, Drop Lead Single

After eight years of silence, Venom are back with their 16th studio album *Into Oblivion*, due this May. The lead single "Lay Down Your Soul" is out now, delivering exactly what you'd expect from the godfathers of black metal. The lineup that recorded *Storm the Gates* in 2018 is intact — same crew, same malice, no apologies.

## Megadeth's Farewell Tour Isn't Done Yet

Megadeth's Canadian run alongside Anthrax and Exodus is winding down, but the farewell machine is nowhere near stopping. The band's self-titled final record dropped January 23, and drummer Dirk Verbeuren has publicly hinted the tour will likely be documented with an official live album. For a farewell projected to span several years, they're treating the archiving with the same seriousness as the shows.

## Exodus Return With *Goliath* on March 20

Exodus drop their 13th studio album *Goliath* via Napalm Records on March 20, marking the return of vocalist Rob Dukes — his first full record with the band since *Exhibit B: The Human Condition* in 2010. Early critical word out of review circles is strong — full-throttle Bay Area thrash with nothing held back. The kind of record Exodus was always supposed to make in their second run.

## Sevendust Premiere "Unbreakable," Album and Tour Locked In

Sevendust dropped the music video for "Unbreakable," the latest preview of their upcoming album *One*, due this May. The band has locked in a full tour alongside Atreyu and Fire From The Gods to support the record. Based on what's previewed so far, *One* is shaping up to be one of the more serious hard rock statements of the spring cycle.

## Unearth Return to Metal Blade Records

Unearth are back at Metal Blade Records — the label where they built their reputation — with new music expected in 2027. Metal Blade A&R Michael Faley called the re-signing "a massive win for both sides." After years at other homes, this feels like a band getting back to the environment where the best work gets made.

## Black Label Society Announce *Engines of Demolition* for March 27

Zakk Wylde and Black Label Society are back with *Engines of Demolition*, dropping March 27 via MNRK Heavy. Standard BLS operating procedure: heavy, blues-drenched, and engineered to shake walls. If you've been riding with Wylde since *Sonic Brew*, this one's already in the pre-order queue.

## Iron Maiden Add Second *Run For Your Lives* Date in Australia

Iron Maiden added a second date to their *Run For Your Lives* Australian run, with general public tickets going on-sale today. The first show sold fast enough that the second announcement came within 24 hours of the initial confirm. Demand for Maiden in the Southern Hemisphere remains exactly what it's always been — immediate and total.
