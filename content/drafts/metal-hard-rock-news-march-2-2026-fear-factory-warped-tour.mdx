## Dino Cazares Shuts Down Fear Factory Reunion — Permanently

On February 28, Dino Cazares posted a statement that should end the reunion conversation for good. After years of lawsuits involving Burton C. Bell, Christian Olde Wolbers, and Raymond Herrera that fractured the lineup beyond repair, Cazares was direct: "What's done is done. That chapter is permanently closed." No emotion, no ambiguity, no door left cracked. The current lineup moves forward. This one is done.

## Warped Tour 2026 Adds Thursday, Varials — and Goes International

The latest Warped Tour 2026 lineup wave adds Thursday and Varials while announcing the festival's first international expansion in years. The circuit now spans five stops: Washington D.C. (June 13–14), Long Beach, CA (July 25–26), Montreal (August 21–22), Mexico City (September 12–13), and Orlando (November 14–15). Canada and Mexico being on the map is a real shift for a festival that spent years locked to U.S. dates.

## Rob Zombie's *The Great Satan* Is Out

Zombie's eighth solo album dropped Friday on Nuclear Blast. *The Great Satan* is being described as a return to Hellbilly roots — punk-fueled, raw, deliberately abrasive. If his recent output felt too produced, this one's the corrective. Worth hearing before writing it off.

## Varials Drop *Where the Light Leaves* With New Vocalist

Varials released their fourth album on Fearless Records Friday — first with new vocalist Skyler Conder. The band calls it their most unfiltered record to date. Conder doesn't try to replicate what came before, and the record benefits from it. If you've been watching where metalcore's harder edge is actually going in 2026, this is relevant.

## March Is Already Stacked

Two flagged releases dropping this month: Lamb of God's *Into Oblivion* and Exodus's *Goliath*. Neither band has phoned in a record, and *Goliath* as a title alone signals intent. Dates will confirm — we'll have both covered when they land.
