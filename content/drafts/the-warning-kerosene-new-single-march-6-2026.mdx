The Warning are done waiting. The Monterrey trio announced **"Kerosene"** will drop this Friday, March 6 — their first new music since *ERROR* dropped in 2022. That's three-plus years of silence from one of the most talked-about acts in rock.

The band posted a teaser six days ago. No album announcement yet, no label press release — just a title, a release date, and a pre-save link. The kind of drop that lets the music do the work.

## What We Know

"Kerosene" arrives via Universal Music. Pre-save is live now at [thewarning.lnk.to/Kerosene](https://thewarning.lnk.to/Kerosene). The teaser running on YouTube clocks in at 13 seconds — enough to confirm something's different in tone. Daniela, Paulina, and Alejandra Villarreal have been building toward this moment since they wrapped the *ERROR* cycle.

The timing isn't accidental. Within two weeks of the single, the band hits:

- **March 13** — Lollapalooza Chile
- **March 15** — Lollapalooza Argentina
- **March 18** — Asuncionico, Paraguay
- **March 20** — Estéreo Picnic, Colombia

A new single right before a Latin American festival circuit is a clean play — drop something fresh, build heat, let the crowd reactions do the rest.

## Three Years Since ERROR

*ERROR* (2022) was the record that pushed The Warning from "viral YouTube sisters" to a legitimate touring force. It landed on major festival stages and opened doors in markets that don't typically embrace rock with this much edge. The gap since has been conspicuous. Kerosene is the first signal of where they're going next.

No album, no tracklist, no touring lineup announced beyond the festival dates. Just Friday.

We'll have more once the track is out.
